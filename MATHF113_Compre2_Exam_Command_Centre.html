01 · Score Architecture

Marks Are Not Spread Evenly

Inference dominates, but the safest marks are not always the highest-weight marks. The winning route is a blend: bank regression and standard intervals, then attack MLE, joint distributions, and Type-II/sample-size parts.

0-45

Bankable Marks

  • Regression normal equations, R², and variance estimate.
  • Basic z/t/proportion/chi-square confidence intervals.
  • Normal probabilities and binomial mean/variance.
  • First two parts of joint pdf questions: k and marginals.
45-100

Core Differentiators

  • MLE/MoM derivations with log-likelihood discipline.
  • Hypothesis tests with p-value and rejection-region language.
  • Conditional expectation in joint distributions.
  • CLT and normal approximation with continuity correction.
100+

Top-End Separators

  • Type-II error, power, and sample size algebra.
  • Gamma/chi-square pivots and non-standard confidence intervals.
  • Proof questions: independence transformations, mutually exclusive vs independent.
  • Custom rejection regions and estimator comparison.

First 60 Minutes

Take the computationally stable questions: regression, standard CI, PMF construction, normal/binomial, and the easy parts of joint pdfs.

Middle 75 Minutes

Do MLE/MoM, full joint distributions, hypothesis-test core parts, and CLT/Poisson process questions.

Last 45 Minutes

Return to Type-II/power, sample size, custom pivots, proof-heavy parts, and arithmetic checks.
